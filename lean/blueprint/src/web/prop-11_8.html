0.1.9 Proposition 11.8: Nonzero and Truncation

[Proposition 11.8] lem:11_3a, lem:11_3b, lem:11_6a, prop:11_7

  1. \(\mathcal{L}_\tau \neq 0\).

  2. \(x_\tau = s \Rightarrow \mathcal{L}_\tau ^+ = 0\) and \(\mathcal{L}_\tau ^- = 0\).

  3. \(x_\tau \in \{ r, c\} \Rightarrow \mathcal{L}_\tau ^+ \neq 0\) and \(\mathcal{L}_\tau ^- = 0\).

  4. \(x_\tau = d \Rightarrow \mathcal{L}_\tau ^+ \neq 0\) and \(\mathcal{L}_\tau ^- \neq 0\).

Proof ▼
lem:11_3a, lem:11_3b, lem:11_6a By induction. From Lemma 11.6(a): \(\mathcal{E}(1) = (p_{\tau _\mathbf {t}}, (-1)^{\varepsilon _\tau } q_{\tau _\mathbf {t}})\).

  • \(x_\tau = s\): \(p_{\tau _\mathbf {t}} = 0\), \(\varepsilon _\tau = 1\), \(q_{\tau _\mathbf {t}} \geq 2\). Truncation \(\Lambda _{(1,0)}\) fails (\(|0| {\lt} 1\)). \(\Lambda _{(0,1)}\) fails (containment check: \(-q_{\tau _\mathbf {t}} \leq -2\), neither \(0 \leq 1 \leq -q\) nor \(-q \leq 1 \leq 0\)).

  • \(x_\tau \in \{ r, c\} \): \(p_{\tau _\mathbf {t}} \geq 1\), \(\varepsilon _\tau = 1\). \(\Lambda _{(1,0)}\) succeeds. \(\Lambda _{(0,1)}\): \(q_1 = -q_{\tau _\mathbf {t}} \leq 0\), fails.

  • \(x_\tau = d\): \(p_{\tau _\mathbf {t}} \geq 1\), \(q_{\tau _\mathbf {t}} \geq 1\), \(\varepsilon _\tau = 0\). Both truncations succeed.
