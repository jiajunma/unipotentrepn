0.1.1 MYD Operations (Section 9.4)

[Sign twist] ILS.twistBD For \(\star \in \{ B, D\} \) and \((\varepsilon ^+, \varepsilon ^-) \in \mathbb {Z}/2\mathbb {Z} \times \mathbb {Z}/2\mathbb {Z}\), the sign twist \(\mathcal{E} \otimes (\varepsilon ^+, \varepsilon ^-)\) acts on odd-length rows by multiplying the entries by powers of \((-1)\).

[Character twist \(T\)] ILS.charTwistCM For \(\star \in \{ C, \tilde{C}\} \), the involution \(T\) negates entries at positions \(i \equiv 2 \pmod{4}\).

[\(T^2 = \mathrm{id}\)] ILS.charTwistCM_involutive def:charTwistCM The character twist \(T\) is an involution: \(T^2 = \mathrm{id}\).

Proof ▼
Each entry is either negated (at positions \(\equiv 2 \pmod{4}\)) or unchanged. Negating twice gives the identity.
Proof ▼

[Augmentation] ILS.augment The augmentation \(\mathcal{E}' \oplus (p_0, q_0)\) prepends \((p_0, q_0)\) at position 1.

[Truncation] ILS.truncate The truncation \(\Lambda _{(p_0,q_0)}(\mathcal{E})\) subtracts \((p_0, q_0)\) from the first entry, defined when the containment condition \(\mathcal{E} \supseteq (p_0, q_0)\) holds.

[Signature] ILS.sign The signature \(\mathrm{Sign}(\mathcal{E}) = (p, q)\) of an ILS.

[Sign twist preserves signature] ILS.twistBD_sign def:twistBD, def:sign \(\mathrm{Sign}(\mathcal{E} \otimes (\varepsilon ^+, \varepsilon ^-)) = \mathrm{Sign}(\mathcal{E})\).

Proof ▼
Twist only changes signs; signature uses absolute values.
Proof ▼

[Character twist preserves signature] ILS.charTwistCM_sign def:charTwistCM, def:sign \(\mathrm{Sign}(T^j(\mathcal{E})) = \mathrm{Sign}(\mathcal{E})\).

Proof ▼
Same argument as sign twist.
Proof ▼

[Augmentation sign formula] ILS.sign_cons_nonneg def:augment, def:sign For \(p_0, q_0 \geq 0\): \(\mathrm{Sign}((p_0, q_0) :: E) = (p_0 + \mathrm{Sign}(E).1 + \mathrm{fcSign}(E).2,\; q_0 + \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 + \mathrm{fcSign}(E).1)\).

Proof ▼
By the shift identity for signAux.
Proof ▼
