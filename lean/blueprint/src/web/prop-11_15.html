0.1.11 Proposition 11.15: Main Bijection

[Lemma 11.13: Injectivity for quasi-distinguished] prop:11_12, lem:11_5a, prop:11_4 If \(\check{\mathcal{O}}\) is quasi-distinguished and \(\mathcal{L}_\tau [_1] = \mathcal{L}_\tau [_2]\), then \(\tau _1 = \tau _2\).

Proof ▼
prop:11_12, lem:11_5a By induction. Operations in (11.11) are invertible, so \(\mathcal{L}_\tau [_1] = \mathcal{L}_\tau [_2]\) implies \(\mathcal{L}_{\tau _1''} = \mathcal{L}_{\tau _2''}\). IH gives \(\tau _1'' = \tau _2''\). Descent bijectivity (Prop 11.4) recovers \(\tau _1 = \tau _2\).
Proof ▼

[Lemma 11.14: Surjectivity] lem:11_5a, prop:11_4 For quasi-distinguished \(\check{\mathcal{O}}\): for all \(\mathcal{E} \in \mathbf{MYD}_\star (\mathcal{O})\), there exist \(\tau \) and \(\varepsilon \) with \(\mathcal{L}_\tau \otimes (\varepsilon , \varepsilon ) = \mathcal{E}\).

Proof ▼
lem:11_5a, prop:11_4 Invert (11.11) to construct \(\tau \) from \(\mathcal{E}\).
Proof ▼

[Proposition 11.15] prop:11_12, lem:11_13, lem:11_14 The map \((\tau , \varepsilon ) \mapsto \mathcal{L}_\tau \otimes (\varepsilon , \varepsilon )\) is injective with image \(\mathbf{MYD}_\star (\mathcal{O})\).

Proof ▼
prop:11_12, lem:11_13, lem:11_14 Injectivity from Prop 11.12 + Lemma 11.13. Surjectivity from Lemma 11.14.
Proof ▼
