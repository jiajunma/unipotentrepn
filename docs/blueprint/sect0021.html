Definition 59 Sign twist \(\otimes (\varepsilon ^+, \varepsilon ^-)\)
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 54

Lean declarations

  • ILS.twistBD
  • ILS.twistBDRow

(Paper Eq. (9.15).) For \((\varepsilon ^+, \varepsilon ^-) \in \mathbb {Z}^2\) (in practice \(\{ \pm 1\} ^2\)), acts only on odd-length rows (\(\ell = i + 1\) odd, \(i\) even):

\[ \mathrm{twistBDRow}(i,\ \varepsilon ^+, \varepsilon ^-)(p, q) := \begin{cases} (p, q) & \ell \text{ even} \\ (\varepsilon ^{+\, h + 1} \varepsilon ^{-\, h}\, p,\ \varepsilon ^{-\, h + 1} \varepsilon ^{+\, h}\, q) & \ell \text{ odd, } h = i / 2. \end{cases} \]

Common cases:

  • \((1, -1)\): sign twist \(\otimes 1^{+-}\) (B/D post-twist when \(\varepsilon _\tau = 1\)).

  • \((-1, -1)\): det twist (C/M pre-twist when \(\varepsilon _\wp = 1\)).

  • \((1, 1)\): identity.

(Paper Eq. (9.16)–(9.17).) For \(j \in \mathbb {Z}\), \(T^j\) negates the pair at row index \(i\) iff \(j\) is odd and \((i + 1) \bmod 4 = 2\):

\[ \mathrm{charTwistCMRow}(j, i)(p, q) := \begin{cases} (-p, -q) & j\text{ odd and } (i + 1) \bmod 4 = 2 \\ (p, q) & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \]
