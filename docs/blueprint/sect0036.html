Lemma 103 Lemma 11.1(b) — abstract bijection
✓

Uses

  • lemma_11_1_b_bijection
  • lemma_11_1_b_bijection_concrete

Abstract form: given finite types \(\alpha \simeq \beta \) of equal cardinality and an injection \(f : \alpha \to \beta \), \(f\) is a bijection.

Proof ▶

A finite type \(\alpha \) with an injection into \(\beta \) and an equivalence \(\alpha \simeq \beta \) satisfies \(|\alpha | = |\beta |\); an injective map between finite sets of equal cardinality is bijective.
