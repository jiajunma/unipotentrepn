Given an ILS \(E\) (intended as \(\mathcal{L}_{\tau ''}\)) with the firstColSign invariant

\[ \mathrm{fcSign}(E) = (\mathrm{Sign}(E).1 - n_{\mathrm{prev}},\ \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 - n_{\mathrm{prev}}) \]

and target tail signature \((p_t, q_t)\) satisfying

\[ p_\tau - 2 n_{\mathrm{inner}} + \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 = p_t,\quad q_\tau - 2 n_{\mathrm{inner}} + \mathrm{Sign}(E).1 = q_t, \]

define \(n_0 := n_{\mathrm{inner}} - \mathrm{Sign}(E).1 - \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 + n_{\mathrm{prev}}\) and \(E_{\mathrm{inner}} := T^{\gamma _1}\big(E \oplus (n_0, n_0)\big)\). Then:

\begin{align*} p_\tau - \mathrm{Sign}(E_{\mathrm{inner}}).1 - \mathrm{fcSign}(E_{\mathrm{inner}}).2 & = p_t, \\ q_\tau - \mathrm{Sign}(E_{\mathrm{inner}}).2 - \mathrm{fcSign}(E_{\mathrm{inner}}).1 & = q_t. \end{align*}

These are the “outer augment” parameters consumed by the outer C\(\to \)D or M\(\to \)B lift.

Proof ▶

Step 1 — unfold sign and fcSign of \(E_{\mathrm{inner}}\). \(T^\gamma \) preserves both sign and fcSign (Lem 64, 65), so we may work with \(F := E \oplus (n_0, n_0) = (n_0, n_0) :: E\). By Lem 66 (for \(n_0 \geq 0\)):

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Sign}(F) & = (n_0 + \mathrm{Sign}(E).1 + \mathrm{fcSign}(E).2,\ n_0 + \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 + \mathrm{fcSign}(E).1). \\ \mathrm{fcSign}(F) & = (n_0 + \mathrm{fcSign}(E).2,\ n_0 + \mathrm{fcSign}(E).1) \end{align*}

using the cons formula for fcSign (the swap is due to parity index shift, Eq. (9.12)).

Substituting \(\mathrm{fcSign}(E) = (\mathrm{Sign}(E).1 - n_{\mathrm{prev}},\ \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 - n_{\mathrm{prev}})\):

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Sign}(F).1 & = n_0 + \mathrm{Sign}(E).1 + \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 - n_{\mathrm{prev}} = n_{\mathrm{inner}} \\ \mathrm{fcSign}(F).2 & = n_0 + \mathrm{Sign}(E).1 - n_{\mathrm{prev}} = n_{\mathrm{inner}} - \mathrm{Sign}(E).2. \end{align*}

where we used the definition \(n_0 = n_{\mathrm{inner}} - \mathrm{Sign}(E).1 - \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 + n_{\mathrm{prev}}\).

Step 2 — substitute into the outer augment formula. \(p_\tau - \mathrm{Sign}(F).1 - \mathrm{fcSign}(F).2 = p_\tau - n_{\mathrm{inner}} - (n_{\mathrm{inner}} - \mathrm{Sign}(E).2) = p_\tau - 2 n_{\mathrm{inner}} + \mathrm{Sign}(E).2 = p_t\) (by hypothesis).

The \(q\)-equation is symmetric.
